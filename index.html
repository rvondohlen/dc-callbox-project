The District Callbox Project

The District Callbox Project was initiated to documant and map the remaining Police and Fire callboxes within the Washington, DC city limits

What is a callbox?

Before telephones, callboxes were used by police and fire departments as well as by the public, to communicate with emergency personnel. They come in various shapes which were representative of their function.

Why map them?

After a bit of research it seems that no one has a compete survey of the callboxes that currently remain in the district. Though historical maps exist and localized efforts have been made to map restoration projects, this is an effort to bring those together while allowing anyone to learn about these historical artifacts and contribute to their preservation.

How can you get involved?

If you have any corrections, questions, or additional information about call boxes, don't hesitate to reach out.-
